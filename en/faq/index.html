International Clients

Yes, a foreign national can register a company in Georgia without traveling there in person. The process can be carried out through an authorized representative on the basis of a duly certified power of attorney.

To benefit from the small business 1% tax regime, the individual must be registered as a sole entrepreneur, their annual income must not exceed 500,000 GEL, the activity must meet the requirements established by Georgian tax legislation, and the activity must not belong to the categories of prohibited activities defined under Georgian tax law.

Foreigners may purchase residential and commercial real estate on equal terms with Georgian citizens. The only restriction is the direct purchase of agricultural land, if there are no exceptions by law.

Requirements vary by bank, but generally a duly certified passport is required, along with completion of the bank's KYC forms and, in the case of remote representation, a duly certified power of attorney.
